4. Information We Collect

A. Website Usage

This website is an informational resource. It does not use marketing cookies or tracking pixels. Technical logs (IP addresses) are processed by our host, GitHub Pages, for security and stability purposes.

B. Clinical Records

When you become a patient, we collect:

  • Personal identifiers (Name, DOB, NHS Number).
  • Contact details (Address, phone, secure email).
  • Medical history, diagnostic reports, imaging (CT/MRI), and pathology results.
  • Private insurance details or billing information.

5. Data Sharing & Third Parties

To provide safe and effective clinical care, your data may be shared with:

  • Your General Practitioner (GP) and referring consultants.
  • Hospital providers (for scans, blood tests, or radiotherapy delivery).
  • Clinical multidisciplinary teams (MDTs) for peer-review of treatment plans.
  • Private medical insurers (for billing and authorisation).
  • Professional clinical auditors or regulators (as required by law).

6. Data Retention

We retain medical records in accordance with the Records Management Code of Practice for Health and Social Care. Generally, clinical records are retained for a minimum of 8 years after the conclusion of treatment, or longer in specific oncological contexts, to ensure continuity of care and legal compliance.

7. Security & Email Communication

Warning: Standard internet email is not a secure medium. Information sent via email could be intercepted. By emailing the Practice, you acknowledge these risks. We recommend the use of encrypted platforms or NHS-to-NHS mail for the transfer of sensitive diagnostic reports.

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Request access to your medical records (Subject Access Request).
  • Request rectification of inaccurate data.
  • Object to processing (noting that this may impact our ability to provide safe care).
  • Lodge a complaint with the ICO (www.ico.org.uk).
