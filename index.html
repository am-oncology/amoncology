Biography & Expertise

Professional Profile

Dr Asad Mahmood is a Consultant Clinical Oncologist at Barts Health NHS Trust, specialising in the management of gastrointestinal and neurological cancers. His practice encompasses both systemic therapies and advanced radiotherapy techniques, including stereotactic radiosurgery/radiotherapy (SRS/SBRT) and contact brachytherapy (Papillon).

He completed a doctoral clinical fellowship at the Francis Crick Institute, earning a PhD in Cancer Metabolism from University College London (UCL). He holds an MSc in Oncology from the Institute of Cancer Research (ICR), where he was awarded the Professor Alan Horwich Prize. He graduated with distinction from Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry, receiving Proxime Accessit to the University of London Gold Medal alongside several academic prizes.

Alongside his clinical work, Dr Mahmood is involved in quality improvement initiatives at Barts, including digital patient monitoring (ePROMs) and sustainable practice (Green Radiotherapy). He serves as a sub-investigator on clinical trials and as a Clinical Educator in Oncology, holding a formal teaching qualification (PGCert/CILT) from Queen Mary University of London. His research spanning laboratory science, data analysis, and clinical outcomes has been published and presented at national and international conferences.
